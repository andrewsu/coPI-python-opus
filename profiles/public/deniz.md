# Ashok Deniz Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Ashok Deniz
**Institution:** Scripps Research Institute

## Research Summary

The Deniz lab at Scripps Research investigates the biophysics of biomolecular condensates, intrinsically disordered proteins (IDPs), and liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) using single-molecule fluorescence approaches as a central methodology. A major thrust of the lab's work has been elucidating how multivalent interactions, electrostatics, and polymer physics govern the formation, material properties, and function of membraneless organelles. Recent work has revealed that biomolecular condensates can induce local membrane potentials through electrostatic interactions with lipid bilayers, with implications for neuronal signaling. The lab has also demonstrated reentrant phase behavior in polyphosphate-DNA condensate systems, showing how DNA shells tune condensate size, and has contributed to understanding the ultrastructural basis of condensate function using the bacterial PopZ system. Earlier foundational work established mechanisms of nucleophosmin (NPM1) phase separation in the nucleolus, divalent cation-mediated switching between heterotypic and homotypic RNA coacervates, and RNA homopolymer condensation. In parallel, the lab has made significant contributions to understanding IDP conformational landscapes, including studies of α-synuclein, CREB, and the adenovirus E1A oncoprotein, revealing allosteric mechanisms in disordered protein interaction networks. Methodologically, the lab pioneers single-molecule FRET techniques, microfluidic mixing, and multicolor smFRET approaches for studying protein folding, binding, and condensate biophysics.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Single-molecule Förster resonance energy transfer (smFRET)
- Ratiometric single-molecule FRET for IDP conformational subpopulation analysis
- Multicolor (three-color) single-molecule FRET
- Ultrafast microfluidic mixing coupled with smFRET
- Confocal fluorescence microscopy for condensate imaging
- Differential interference contrast (DIC) microscopy
- Giant unilamellar vesicle (GUV) preparation and electrochromic dye-based membrane potential measurements
- Turbidity and UV-vis absorbance assays for phase separation
- Small-angle X-ray scattering (SAXS)
- NMR spectroscopy for IDP structural characterization
- Analytical ultracentrifugation
- Cryo-electron tomography (cryo-ET)
- Molecular dynamics simulations
- Ultrafast temperature-jump spectroscopy with sapphire nano-coating substrates
- Fluorescence labeling via unnatural amino acid incorporation and click chemistry
- Numerical electro-thermodynamic modeling of condensate-membrane interfaces
