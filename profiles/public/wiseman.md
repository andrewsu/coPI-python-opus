# Luke Wiseman Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Luke Wiseman
**Institution:** The Scripps Research Institute
**Department:** Department of Molecular Medicine

## Research Summary

The Wiseman lab at The Scripps Research Institute investigates the mechanisms by which stress-responsive signaling pathways—particularly the unfolded protein response (UPR)—regulate protein homeostasis (proteostasis) in both intracellular and extracellular environments, and how these mechanisms deteriorate during aging and disease. A central focus is the ATF6 arm of the UPR, where the lab has established that pharmacological activation of ATF6 can reduce secretion of destabilized, amyloidogenic proteins such as immunoglobulin light chains implicated in light chain amyloidosis (AL) and transthyretin linked to systemic amyloidoses. Using quantitative interactome proteomics, the lab has defined the molecular basis by which ATF6 activation retains misfolded proteins in the ER through enhanced engagement with pro-folding chaperones. Complementary work has revealed roles for PERK signaling in regulating extracellular proteostasis and a non-canonical structural function for IRE1α at mitochondria-associated ER membranes (MAMs) that controls calcium transfer and bioenergetics. The lab also studies mitochondrial quality control through the AAA+ protease AFG3L2, using cryo-EM to define how disease-linked mutations impair its function in neurodegeneration. Ongoing efforts integrate small molecule discovery, targeted RNA sequencing platforms for pathway deconvolution, and aging models in C. elegans to develop therapeutic strategies that restore proteostasis capacity across tissues in age-related and amyloid diseases.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- quantitative interactome proteomics (co-immunoprecipitation coupled with mass spectrometry)
- cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM) structure determination
- targeted RNA sequencing (RNAseq) for stress-responsive pathway deconvolution
- high-throughput screening (HTS) with cell-based reporter assays
- site-directed mutagenesis and recombinant protein expression
- RNAscope in situ hybridization
- Sanger sequencing and genetic variant characterization
- conformational reporters for secreted amyloidogenic proteins
- pharmacologic and genetic protein stabilization approaches
- qPCR-based transcriptional activity assays
- electroretinography for functional vision assessment
- small molecule screening for stress-responsive signaling activators
- C. elegans mitochondrial proteomics (global and tissue-specific)

## Model Systems

- C. elegans (age-related mitochondrial proteome studies)
- IRE1α conditional knockout mice (liver metabolism studies)
- HEK293 and related mammalian cell lines for UPR reporter assays
- human retinal tissue (RNAscope analysis of ATF6/ATF6B expression)
- patient-derived genetic samples (achromatopsia families with ATF6 mutations)
- amyloidogenic immunoglobulin light chain (LC) secretion cell models
- transthyretin (TTR) conformational reporter cell systems
- recombinant human AFG3L2 catalytic core for cryo-EM
