# Michael Petrascheck Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Michael Petrascheck
**Institution:** Scripps Research Institute
**Department:** Molecular and Cellular Biology

## Research Summary

The Petrascheck lab investigates the molecular mechanisms of aging and age-related disease using a distinctive chemical biology approach that integrates high-throughput pharmacological screening in C. elegans with mechanistic validation in mammalian systems. A central theme is the discovery of geroprotective compounds—small molecules that extend lifespan—and the elucidation of their molecular targets, leveraging activity-based protein profiling and Bayesian inference methods (TargetSeeker-MS) to identify drug-target interactions on a proteome-wide scale. Recent work has employed graph neural networks to rationally design polypharmacological geroprotectors targeting multiple biogenic amine receptors, achieving exceptional efficacy in lifespan extension. The lab has uncovered key roles for lipid metabolism (endocannabinoid-related pathways, sphingolipid imbalance, epoxide metabolism), mitochondrial ATP synthase, autophagy, and mRNA translation regulation in aging and proteostasis. A parallel line of investigation explores how energy balance and food intake are regulated, identifying targets such as CLIC1, V-ATPase (ATP6v0a1), and serotonergic receptors that control feeding behavior, fat storage, and antipsychotic-induced hyperphagia. Additional work examines how sensory perception modulates aging and disease, and how activity-dependent protein synthesis—particularly of Emerin—gates neuronal plasticity through proteostatic mechanisms. Together, these efforts connect aging biology, neurodegeneration, obesity, and dietary restriction through shared molecular pathways amenable to pharmacological intervention.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- High-throughput C. elegans lifespan screening in 96-well microtiter plates
- Bacterial clearance assay for quantifying C. elegans food intake
- Activity-based protein profiling (ABPP) with covalent inhibitor libraries
- TargetSeeker-MS: Bayesian inference for drug-target discovery using energetics-based protein separation coupled to mass spectrometry
- Graph neural networks for predicting polypharmacological geroprotectors
- Metabolic labeling of nascent proteomes (BONCAT/metabolic pulse labeling)
- LC-MS/MS metabolomics (HILIC, Q Exactive hybrid quadrupole orbitrap)
- Transcriptomic analysis of aging tissues (RNA-seq, gene expression profiling)
- Network analysis integrating metabolomics and transcriptomics data
- ATP bioluminescence assays for mitochondrial function
- Mouse lifespan and metabolic phenotyping studies
- Electrophysiological recording of visually evoked responses in mice
- Dendritic spine morphology analysis
- Adipocyte-specific conditional knockout mouse models
- Phenotypic drug screening in C. elegans for feeding and lifespan modulation
