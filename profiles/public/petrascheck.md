# Michael Petrascheck Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Michael Petrascheck
**Institution:** Scripps Research Institute
**Department:** Molecular and Cellular Biology

## Research Summary

The Petrascheck lab investigates the molecular mechanisms of aging and age-related disease using a chemical biology approach centered on Caenorhabditis elegans as a primary model system. A foundational contribution was the development of high-throughput lifespan screening assays in 96-well microtiter plates, enabling the screening of tens of thousands of small molecules for longevity-extending activity. This work led to the landmark discovery that the antidepressant mianserin extends C. elegans lifespan through serotonergic signaling pathways that mimic dietary restriction. Subsequent studies have expanded into the pharmacological network of lifespan-extending compounds, identifying biogenic amine receptors as key druggable nodes and revealing that neuronal signaling non-cell-autonomously regulates organismal stress responses and proteostasis. The lab has made important contributions to understanding how aging disrupts transcriptional regulation ('transcriptional drift') and metabolic homeostasis ('metabolic drift'), particularly in the brain. More recently, the lab has focused on the mitochondrial ATP synthase as a shared target for aging and dementia, identified α-ketoglutarate as a longevity metabolite acting through ATP synthase inhibition and TOR signaling, and explored how mRNA translation and proteostasis mechanisms intersect with aging and neurodegeneration. Current research directions include dietary restriction mimetics, sensory perception modulation for age-related disease, epoxide metabolism in aging, and the role of protein aggregation in neurodegenerative conditions including prion disease and Alzheimer's disease.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- High-throughput small-molecule lifespan screening in C. elegans using 96-well and 384-well microtiter plates
- Bacterial clearance assays for quantifying C. elegans food intake
- Metabolic pulse-labeling for measuring de novo protein synthesis and nutrient absorption
- Global untargeted metabolomics using LC-MS (HILIC, Q Exactive hybrid quadrupole orbitrap)
- Quantitative proteomics and nascent proteome profiling
- Drug affinity responsive target stability (DARTS) for small-molecule target identification
- TargetSeeker-MS: Bayesian inference for drug-target discovery using energetics-based protein separation coupled to mass spectrometry
- Transcriptomics and transcriptional drift analysis
- RNA-seq and gene expression profiling across aging time courses
- Network and pharmacological pathway analysis
- ATP bioluminescence assays
- C. elegans genetic epistasis and mutant lifespan analysis
- Automated liquid handling for phenotypic drug screening
- Electrophysiological recording of visually evoked responses in mice
- Dendritic spine morphology analysis
- Confocal and fluorescence imaging of C. elegans and mammalian neurons
