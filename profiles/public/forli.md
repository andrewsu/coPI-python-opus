# Stefano Forli Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Stefano Forli

## Research Summary

The Forli lab specializes in computational structural biology and molecular docking, with a central focus on developing and advancing the AutoDock suite of software tools for drug discovery. Over the past decade, the lab has driven major innovations in GPU-accelerated molecular docking (AutoDock-GPU), scoring function development, and methods for handling challenging molecular systems including macrocycles, covalent inhibitors, and peptides. A key recent contribution is the reactive docking protocol, which models covalent ligand-target interactions for virtual chemoproteomics and has been validated across hundreds of complexes involving diverse warheads and nucleophilic residues. The lab has also developed specialized tools for hydration thermodynamics (WaterKit), virtual screening data management (Ringtail), hydrogen bond anisotropy characterization, and bias-enhanced docking protocols. Collaborative efforts have applied these computational methods to diverse biological problems, including structure-based design of covalent kinase inhibitors, HIV integrase strand-transfer inhibitor binding, amyloidogenic light chain stabilization, PPARγ drug-metabolite interactions, and characterization of the orphan receptor GPR158 as a metabotropic glycine receptor. The lab has also evaluated emerging structural prediction tools like AlphaFold2 for docking applications and developed machine learning classifiers to improve peptide docking accuracy. This work bridges computational method development with translational drug discovery, enabling large-scale virtual screening campaigns across the ligandable proteome.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- GPU-accelerated molecular docking (AutoDock-GPU, AutoDock Vina)
- Reactive docking for covalent ligand-target modeling
- High-throughput virtual screening (HTVS) of large compound libraries
- Grid-based explicit water thermodynamic profiling (WaterKit/GIST)
- Macrocyclic ligand flexible docking with on-the-fly conformer sampling
- Random forest classifiers for peptide docking pose discrimination
- Quantum mechanics calculations for hydrogen bond anisotropy mapping
- Molecular dynamics simulations
- Replica exchange molecular dynamics (REMD) and asynchronous replica exchange (AsyncRE)
- MM-GBSA binding free energy rescoring
- Coarse-grained integrative modeling of ribonucleoprotein complexes
- Monte Carlo peptide docking with crankshaft backbone sampling (ADCP)
- Bias-enhanced docking using pharmacophore and interaction constraints (AutoDock Bias)
- SQLite database management for virtual screening results (Ringtail)
- AutoGrid4 affinity map generation
- Gradient-based local search optimization (ADADELTA)
- Ligand preparation with Meeko and OpenBabel
- Python scripting and bindings for docking workflow automation
- Cryo-EM structure interpretation and docking target evaluation
- AlphaFold2 predicted structure evaluation for docking applications
