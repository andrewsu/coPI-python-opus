# Danielle Grotjahn Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Danielle Grotjahn

## Research Summary

The Grotjahn lab investigates the structural and functional organization of organellar membranes, with a primary focus on mitochondrial dynamics, using cryo-electron tomography (cryo-ET) as a central imaging modality. A major contribution of the lab has been the development of the Surface Morphometrics pipeline, an open-source computational framework for quantifying membrane ultrastructure—including curvature, spacing, and thickness—from cryo-ET data. Recent work has applied this pipeline to reveal how organellar membrane thickness varies across subcompartments and correlates with local curvature, and to characterize how cytoplasmic ribosomes engage the outer mitochondrial membrane to facilitate co-translational protein import in yeast. The lab also investigates the interplay between mitochondrial morphology and stress-responsive signaling, demonstrating that pharmacologic activation of integrated stress response (ISR) kinases such as HRI and GCN2 can counteract pathologic mitochondrial fragmentation caused by disease-relevant insults, including MFN2 mutations associated with neurological dysfunction. Additional collaborative work has explored mitochondrial DNA replication stress and its connection to innate immune signaling via cGAS-STING, the role of cytoskeletal filaments including actin and septins in mitochondrial constriction, and PERK-dependent phospholipid remodeling that promotes mitochondrial elongation during ER stress. Collectively, the lab bridges structural cell biology and organellar stress biology, leveraging cryo-ET and computational quantification to connect nanoscale membrane architecture with cellular physiology and disease.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Cellular cryo-electron tomography (cryo-ET)
- Subtomogram averaging of macromolecular complexes in situ
- Surface morphometrics pipeline for membrane ultrastructure quantification
- Membrane surface mesh reconstruction and geometric analysis (curvature, thickness, spacing)
- Deep learning-based segmentation of cryo-ET data
- Correlative cryo-fluorescence microscopy and cryo-ET (cryo-CLEM)
- Tilt-series reconstruction and tomogram processing (SART, RELION)
- Template matching for particle picking in tomograms
- Cryo-focused ion beam (cryo-FIB) milling for lamella preparation
- Pharmacologic perturbation of ISR kinases (HRI, GCN2 activators)
- Confocal fluorescence microscopy for mitochondrial network morphology assessment
- Exome sequencing and bioinformatics variant analysis
- Primary human fibroblast derivation and culture

- Saccharomyces cerevisiae (budding yeast)
- Mammalian cell lines (HeLa, U2OS, MEFs)
- Patient-derived fibroblasts expressing MFN2 D414V pathogenic variant
- Human neuroblastoma SK-N-SH cells (flavivirus infection model)
- TFAM-depleted cells (mtDNA replication stress model)
- MFN2-deficient cells
- Herpes simplex virus-1 (HSV-1) infection model
- Tick-borne encephalitis virus (TBEV) infection model
- In vitro reconstituted polyphosphate-DNA-Mg2+ condensate system
- Mouse brain tissue (cryo-ET of viral replication)
- Danio rerio (zebrafish) embryos (earlier developmental biology work)
