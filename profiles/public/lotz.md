# Martin Lotz Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Martin Lotz
**Institution:** The Scripps Research Institute
**Department:** Molecular and Cellular Medicine and Dept of Molecular Medicine

## Research Summary

The Lotz laboratory at The Scripps Research Institute investigates the molecular mechanisms underlying joint aging, osteoarthritis (OA) pathogenesis, and cartilage biology, with a particular emphasis on transcription factor networks that regulate tissue homeostasis and degeneration. A central theme of the lab's work is the role of FoxO transcription factors in joint aging and OA, extending this focus to intervertebral disc aging, where FoxO proteins serve as critical regulators of cellular stress responses and tissue maintenance. Recent efforts have expanded to characterize KLF4 as a key factor in joint degradation and regeneration, broadening the understanding of transcriptional programs that balance catabolic and anabolic processes in articular cartilage. The lab employs integrative multi-omics approaches to profile human cartilage during aging and OA, leveraging transcriptomic, proteomic, and epigenomic datasets to define disease-associated molecular signatures. A significant newer direction involves high-resolution three-dimensional mapping of cellular heterogeneity within mineralized tissues and mapping the joint-nerve interactome of the knee, reflecting an interest in tissue architecture, innervation, and neuro-skeletal crosstalk. These studies integrate spatial profiling technologies with single-cell analyses to uncover previously unappreciated cellular diversity and communication networks in joint tissues, positioning the lab at the interface of aging biology, musculoskeletal disease, and systems-level tissue mapping.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Integrative multi-omics analysis (transcriptomics, proteomics, epigenomics)
- Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) of joint tissues
- Spatial transcriptomics and high-resolution 3D tissue mapping
- Histomorphometric and immunohistochemical analysis of cartilage and bone
- Conditional and tissue-specific knockout mouse models
- Bulk RNA-seq and ChIP-seq for transcription factor binding analysis
- 3D imaging of mineralized tissues
- Interactome mapping of joint-nerve networks
- In vitro chondrocyte culture and functional assays
- Bioinformatic integration of multi-omics datasets

## Model Systems

- Human articular cartilage explants (aging and OA donors)
- FoxO conditional knockout mice
- KLF4 conditional knockout and overexpression mouse models
- Murine models of osteoarthritis (e.g., destabilization of the medial meniscus)
- Human intervertebral disc tissue
- Primary human chondrocytes
- Murine knee joint tissues for joint-nerve interactome studies
- Mineralized tissue samples (bone, calcified cartilage)

## Key Molecular Targets

- FoxO transcription factors (FoxO1, FoxO3, FoxO4)
- KLF4 (Krüppel-like factor 4)
- Cartilage extracellular matrix components (aggrecan, type II collagen)
- Matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) and ADAMTS enzymes
- Joint-nerve signaling mediators
- mTOR and autophagy pathways in cartilage
