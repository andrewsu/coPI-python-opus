# Andrew Ward Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Andrew Ward

## Research Summary

The Ward lab is a leading structural biology group focused on the architecture of viral surface glycoproteins and the antibody responses they elicit, with the overarching goal of enabling rational vaccine design. Using cryo-electron microscopy as a central platform, the lab determines high-resolution structures of envelope glycoproteins from HIV-1, SARS-CoV-2, influenza (including novel fish/amphibian influenza-like viruses), and filoviruses, both alone and in complex with neutralizing antibodies. A major thrust involves HIV broadly neutralizing antibody (bnAb) development, where the lab has contributed critically to germline-targeting vaccine strategies—from immunogen design and structural validation through preclinical animal models to phase 1 human clinical trials demonstrating bnAb precursor priming and maturation. The lab has pioneered cryo-EM-based polyclonal epitope mapping (EMPEM) to deconvolve serum antibody responses and has developed computational pipelines coupling cryo-EM density interpretation with B cell receptor sequencing for rapid monoclonal antibody discovery. Additional work encompasses structure-guided drug discovery against ion channels (OTOP1), STING innate immune signaling, and antibody language model development. The lab also investigates influenza immunity across subtypes (H1, H5, H7) and lineages (influenza B), informing universal vaccine strategies. Collectively, the Ward lab integrates structural, immunological, and computational approaches to translate atomic-level understanding of antigen-antibody interactions into next-generation vaccines and therapeutics.
