# Andrew Ward Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Andrew Ward

## Research Summary

The Ward lab is a leading structural biology group focused on the architecture of viral surface glycoproteins and the antibody responses they elicit, with the overarching goal of enabling rational vaccine design. Using cryo-electron microscopy as a central platform, the lab determines high-resolution structures of envelope glycoproteins from HIV-1, SARS-CoV-2, influenza (including novel fish/amphibian influenza-like viruses), and filoviruses, both alone and in complex with neutralizing antibodies. A major thrust involves HIV broadly neutralizing antibody (bnAb) development, where the lab has contributed critically to germline-targeting vaccine strategies—from immunogen design and structural validation through preclinical animal models to phase 1 human clinical trials demonstrating bnAb precursor priming and maturation. The lab has pioneered cryo-EM-based polyclonal epitope mapping (EMPEM) to deconvolve serum antibody responses and has developed computational pipelines coupling cryo-EM density interpretation with B cell receptor sequencing for rapid monoclonal antibody discovery. Additional work encompasses structure-guided drug discovery against ion channels (OTOP1), STING innate immune signaling, and antibody language model development. The lab also investigates influenza immunity across subtypes (H1, H5, H7) and lineages (influenza B), informing universal vaccine strategies. Collectively, the Ward lab integrates structural, immunological, and computational approaches to translate atomic-level understanding of antigen-antibody interactions into next-generation vaccines and therapeutics.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Single-particle cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM) of glycoprotein-antibody complexes
- Cryo-EM-based polyclonal epitope mapping (EMPEM) of serum antibodies
- X-ray crystallography of hemagglutinin and envelope glycoproteins
- Negative-stain electron microscopy for nanoparticle and trimer characterization
- Molecular docking-based virtual screening for small molecule discovery
- Whole-cell patch-clamp electrophysiology for ion channel functional testing
- Glycan microarray binding assays
- Mass spectrometry-based glycosylation profiling of viral glycoproteins
- mRNA-lipid nanoparticle (mRNA-LNP) immunogen design and delivery
- Self-assembling protein nanoparticle (SApNP) design and display
- Single-cell RNA sequencing and B cell receptor (BCR) sequencing
- Antibody language model training and scaling law derivation for paired antibody sequences
- ModelAngelo-based automated structure building and hidden Markov model sequence inference
- Molecular dynamics simulations and multi-scale modeling of antibody-membrane interactions
- Coarse-grained and all-atom simulations of protein-lipid interactions
- Hemagglutination inhibition assays and pseudovirus neutralization assays
- Recombinant protein expression in CHO and Expi293F cell systems
- Baculovirus/Sf9 insect cell expression system
- Immunogenicity evaluation in mouse, rabbit, and nonhuman primate models
- Structure-guided immunogen design and germline-targeting antigen engineering
- Reverse genetics system for recombinant influenza virus rescue

## Model Systems

- Rhesus macaques (Indian-origin) for HIV and influenza immunogenicity studies
- Cynomolgus macaques for influenza HA stem immunization
- Knockin (KI) mouse models bearing unmutated common ancestor bnAb B cells (V2 apex V033-a)
- Preclinical mouse models with physiologically relevant bnAb precursor B cell frequencies
- BALB/c and C57BL/6 mice for vaccine immunogenicity and challenge studies
- Rabbit models for HIV Env immunogenicity evaluation
- HEK293T cells for recombinant protein production and transfection
- Expi293F cells for recombinant antibody and glycoprotein expression
- MDCK-SIAT1 cells for influenza virus propagation
- Sf9 insect cells for baculovirus-based protein expression
- CHO cell platform for GMP-scale nanoparticle immunogen manufacturing
- Human phase 1 clinical trial participants (IAVI G002, G003, and BG505 SOSIP.v4.1-GT1.1 trials)
- Human sera from HIV-1-infected cohorts (IAVI Protocol G)
- Murine immune sera from influenza vaccination
- Zebrafish OTOP1 for cryo-EM structure determination and electrophysiology
- HIV-like lipid bilayer membrane models for molecular dynamics simulations
