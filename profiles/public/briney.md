# Bryan Briney Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Bryan Briney
**Institution:** Scripps Research Institute

## Research Summary

The Briney lab at Scripps Research investigates the human antibody repertoire using a combination of high-throughput single-cell genomics, computational immunology, and machine learning approaches. A central theme is understanding the diversity, development, and evolution of human B cell and antibody repertoires, from early bone marrow lymphopoiesis through mature circulating responses. Recent work has produced detailed single-cell atlases of early B cell development, revealing previously undescribed proliferative bursts during BCR recombination, and longitudinal studies tracking antibody repertoire drift over multiple years in healthy donors. A major current focus is the development and optimization of antibody language models (AbLMs), including innovations in curriculum learning, preferential masking of non-templated CDR3 regions, and training strategies that integrate natively paired heavy-light chain sequence data. These computational advances are complemented by structural and immunological studies, including AI-driven protein structure prediction (GhostFold), conformational ensemble-based vaccine design for Lassa virus, and the isolation and characterization of neutralizing monoclonal antibodies against Zika virus. The lab bridges experimental immunology—encompassing single-cell RNA-seq, flow cytometry, and antigen-barcoded B cell sorting—with large-scale computational methods for antibody sequence analysis, protein design, and language model pre-training, positioning it at the intersection of systems immunology and AI-driven biologics discovery.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Single-cell RNA sequencing (10X Chromium 5' scRNA-seq)
- Single-cell immune repertoire profiling (VDJ sequencing)
- Antibody language model pre-training and fine-tuning (masked language modeling, curriculum learning)
- AlphaFold2-based protein structure prediction with MSA subsampling (subsampled AF2, GhostFold)
- ProteinMPNN-based protein sequence redesign
- Flow cytometry and fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS)
- DNA-barcoded antigen bait preparation for B cell sorting
- Next-generation sequencing of antibody heavy and light chain genes
- Recombinant antibody cloning, expression, and purification (IgG reformatting, mammalian transient expression)
- Cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM) structure determination
- Virus neutralization assays and antibody-dependent enhancement assays
- ELISA and antibody binding/competition assays
- Identity-based sequence clustering at scale (MMseqs2-based pipelines)
- Longitudinal antibody repertoire analysis and clonotype tracking
- K18-hACE2 transgenic mouse challenge models for viral protection studies
- Density-gradient centrifugation for PBMC isolation

## Model Systems

- Healthy human donor peripheral blood (PBMCs, memory B cells, plasmablasts)
- SARS-CoV-2 seropositive donor PBMCs
- ZIKV-infected and DENV-experienced donor PBMCs
- K18-hACE2 transgenic mice
- VRC01-gH transgenic mice
- huVH1-2 KI mice
- C57BL/6J mice
- Vero cells for virus propagation
- Human bone marrow-derived B cell subsets (pro-B, pre-B, immature B)
- Large-scale antibody sequence databases (~451M human antibody sequences, Observed Antibody Space)
- Paired antibody sequence datasets (~1.6 million natively paired sequences)
- AntiRef clustered reference datasets
