# Megan Ken Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Megan Ken
**Institution:** Scripps Research Institute
**Department:** Integrative Structural and Computational Biology

## Research Summary

The Ken lab at Scripps Research Institute investigates RNA structural dynamics and their functional consequences in cellular contexts, with a particular emphasis on developing predictive models that connect RNA conformational ensembles to biological activity. A central theme of the lab's work is characterizing how RNAs populate multiple conformational states—including low-abundance, short-lived excited states—and how these dynamic equilibria govern RNA function in vitro and in vivo. Using NMR spectroscopy combined with molecular dynamics simulations, the lab has pioneered ensemble-based approaches to RNA-targeted drug discovery, demonstrating that high-resolution dynamic RNA ensembles can dramatically improve virtual screening performance against targets such as the HIV-1 transactivation response element (TAR). Recent work has extended these principles to SARS-CoV-2 RNA elements, integrating computational structural modeling (FARFAR2) with NMR-based screening to identify small molecules targeting viral RNAs. The lab has also developed experimental frameworks for assessing RNA conformational equilibria within functional cellular environments, showing that excited state populations measured in vitro can predict cellular activity. Ongoing efforts focus on building predictive models of viral RNA cellular behavior and deciphering principles of selective small molecule recognition of complex RNAs, bridging integrative structural biology with translational RNA-targeted therapeutic development.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- NMR spectroscopy (1H, 13C, 15N) for RNA structure determination and chemical shift mapping
- Molecular dynamics simulations for RNA ensemble generation
- Ensemble-based virtual screening of small molecule libraries against RNA targets
- FARFAR2 (Fragment Assembly of RNA with Full-Atom Refinement 2) structural modeling
- Cryo-electron microscopy of RNA constructs
- In vitro transcription and denaturing polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis (dPAGE) purification of RNA
- Temperature replica exchange Langevin dynamics simulations
- Coarse-grained RNA modeling (TOPRNA)
- High-throughput experimental screening of drug-like small molecule libraries against RNA
- Cell-based functional assays for RNA activity (TAR-dependent transactivation, RRE-dependent viral export)
- Crosslink-ligation sequencing data analysis (PARIS, LIGR, SPLASH, COMRADES, hiCLIP)
- Optimized STAR alignment for RNA structure probing data
- Anion-exchange chromatography (Hi-Trap Q) for RNA purification
- Chemical mapping of RNA structure
- Interhelical Euler angle analysis of RNA conformational states
