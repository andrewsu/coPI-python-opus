# Lisa Racki Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Lisa Racki
**Institution:** Scripps Research Institute
**Department:** Integrative Structural and Computational Biology

## Research Summary

The Racki lab investigates the biophysical and cellular functions of polyphosphate (polyP), an ancient inorganic biopolymer found across all domains of life, with a particular focus on how polyP affects bacterial chromatin organization and cytoplasmic dynamics. Using the opportunistic pathogen Pseudomonas aeruginosa as a primary model system, the lab has uncovered fundamental principles governing polyP granule biogenesis during starvation, including the temporal coupling of granule formation to cell cycle exit and the role of the histone H1-like protein AlgP in regulating granule spatial organization. Recent work has revealed that polyP forms condensates whose size is tuned by reentrant DNA shells in magnesium-dependent phase separation, establishing a biophysical framework for understanding the intimate relationship between polyP and the bacterial nucleoid. The lab has further demonstrated that polyP profoundly impacts cytoplasmic mobility and chromosomal dynamics under nitrogen starvation. Combining quantitative fluorescence microscopy, in vitro reconstitution of polyP-DNA-Mg²⁺ condensate systems, and bacterial genetics, the Racki lab bridges biophysical chemistry with microbial cell biology. Prior to establishing an independent research program focused on polyP, Dr. Racki made significant contributions to understanding ATP-dependent chromatin remodeling, particularly the dimeric motor mechanism of the ISWI-class remodeler ACF and the role of histone modifications in regulating remodeling enzyme activity.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Quantitative fluorescence microscopy of live bacteria
- Phase contrast and widefield fluorescence imaging on agarose pads
- In vitro reconstitution of polyP-DNA-Mg²⁺ condensate systems
- Polyphosphate granule enrichment and protein identification
- Bacterial genetics (gene knockouts, targetron mutagenesis, complementation assays)
- Rhamnose- and arabinose-inducible expression systems in bacteria
- Electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) spectroscopy with spin-labeled nucleotide analogs
- Single-particle electron microscopy and 3D reconstruction
- Recombinant histone preparation and methyl-lysine analog installation
- MOPS-buffered minimal media starvation assays (nitrogen and carbon limitation)
- Particle tracking and cytoplasmic mobility measurements in bacteria

## Model Systems

- Pseudomonas aeruginosa (wild-type and polyP-deficient mutants)
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa AlgP deletion and truncation mutants
- Escherichia coli HMS174(DE3) and Keio collection deletion strains
- In vitro polyP-Mg²⁺-DNA condensate reconstitution systems
- Recombinant nucleosomes with site-specific histone modifications
- Arabidopsis thaliana defense signaling mutants (npr1, pad4, eds5, sid2, nahG, cpr1, cpr6)
