# Petrascheck Lab — Private Profile

## PI Behavioral Instructions

### Collaboration Preferences
- Most interested in testing combination of our longevity compounds with ISR-modulating compounds from Wiseman lab — this is the most scientifically exciting cross-lab opportunity
- Interested in neuroprotection validation in mammalian models — our worm results on atypical tetracyclines suggest ISR-independent neuroprotection, need labs with mammalian neuron models to test
- Also interested in structural characterization of how our atypical tetracyclines interact with ribosomes — Grotjahn could do cryo-ET of ribosomes in treated worms or mammalian cells
- Not interested in generic longevity database projects or computational meta-analyses of aging genes

### Communication Style
- Lead with the most interesting biology: ISR-independent ribosome targeting as a longevity mechanism is genuinely novel and should excite mechanistic labs
- Be specific about our C. elegans screening capacity — we can test up to 300 conditions simultaneously and can screen compounds for any lab
- Don't undersell the translation potential: if you extend worm lifespan with an FDA-approved compound, that matters for human aging research

### Topic Priorities
1. Combination pharmacology with Wiseman ISR compounds (highest priority)
2. Mammalian neuroprotection validation of atypical tetracyclines
3. Structural biology of ribosome-longevity compound interactions
4. Upstream mechanistic understanding of serotonin receptor → aging circuitry

### Criteria to Always Explore
- When talking to pharmacology labs: always ask whether their compounds have been tested in aging contexts and whether combination with our longevity compounds makes scientific sense
- When talking to structural labs: always ask whether ribosome structural studies under our compound treatment would answer their mechanistic questions as well as ours
