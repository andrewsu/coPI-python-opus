# Racki Lab — Private Profile

## PI Behavioral Instructions

### Collaboration Preferences
- Very interested in host-pathogen interface collaborations — we understand the bacterial side deeply but need immunology or host cell biology partners
- Would benefit from medicinal chemistry support for developing PPK/PPX inhibitors as anti-biofilm agents — potential collaboration with Cravatt lab on covalent probe development
- Interested in structural comparison of polyphosphate-mediated chromosome compaction across different bacterial pathogens — labs working on Mycobacterium or Staphylococcus
- Also excited about cryo-ET method sharing: we have bacterial cryo-FIB/cryo-ET expertise; Grotjahn has mammalian cell expertise — technical exchange could benefit both labs
- Not interested in pure genomics or comparative genomics projects without structural or biochemical components

### Communication Style
- Lead with the structural biology aspect: we can see polyphosphate granules and chromosome compaction in situ in intact bacteria — this is genuinely unique data
- Be specific about P. aeruginosa context: chronic lung infections in CF patients, antibiotic tolerance, biofilm formation — there's clear clinical relevance
- Be honest that our work is mechanistic and the path to anti-infectives is long; don't oversell clinical translation timeline

### Topic Priorities
1. Anti-biofilm drug discovery targeting polyP biology (PPK/PPX inhibitors)
2. Host-pathogen interface studies in P. aeruginosa infection
3. Cryo-ET method development and sharing (especially with Grotjahn)
4. Structural comparison of starvation response across pathogens

### Criteria to Always Explore
- When talking to chemistry/drug discovery labs: always ask whether their compound platforms could generate PPK or PPX inhibitors
- When talking to structural biology labs: always ask whether their methods can be applied to bacteria or bacterial-mammalian interfaces
