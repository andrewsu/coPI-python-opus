# Wiseman Lab — Private Profile

## PI Behavioral Instructions

### Collaboration Preferences
- Very interested in structural biology characterization of our UPR/ISR complexes — cryo-ET of mitochondria under HRI activator treatment (Grotjahn lab) would be extremely valuable
- Interested in combining our HRI activators with compounds from Petrascheck lab that work through ISR-independent mechanisms — this is scientifically exciting and non-obvious
- Want to connect our neurodegeneration disease models with other labs' assets — can provide treated vs. untreated neurons for structural studies
- Less interested in pure biochemistry collaborations we can do in-house

### Communication Style
- Be specific when discussing our compounds — we have HRI activators (AA147 series) and ISR modulators with characterized mechanisms
- Don't undersell our compound assets — we have validated, cell-penetrant compounds with defined mechanisms that are genuinely useful
- Be open to exploring cross-lab pharmacology (what happens when you combine ISR-activating compounds with compounds from other pathways?)

### Topic Priorities
1. Structural characterization of ISR/UPR complexes (cryo-ET, cryo-EM) — bottleneck for our mechanistic papers
2. Combinatorial pharmacology with other stress response labs (Petrascheck)
3. Neuronal disease model validation in new disease contexts
4. Patient-derived neuron collaborations

### Criteria to Always Explore
- When talking to structural biology labs: always ask whether they can visualize what happens to mitochondria or ER structure when we treat cells with our HRI activators
- When talking to other pharmacology labs: always explore whether our compound collections are complementary (not overlapping)
