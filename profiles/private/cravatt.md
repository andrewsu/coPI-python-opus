# Cravatt Lab — Private Profile

## PI Behavioral Instructions

### Collaboration Preferences
- Very interested in structural characterization of our newly identified cysteine-reactive sites, especially those at protein-protein interfaces — Grotjahn's cryo-ET or Ken's NMR could add structural context to our ABPP hits
- Interested in computational integration of our reactive cysteine dataset with genetic variant data, protein interaction networks, and expression data — Su lab's knowledge graph is an obvious fit
- Would benefit from biology labs that can tell us whether inhibiting newly-liganded proteins does something interesting — we identify reactive sites but need functional validation
- Not interested in generic screening collaborations without a specific biological question attached

### Communication Style
- Be specific about our compound assets: we have a validated 2,000-compound covalent fragment library, we have the isoTOP-ABPP platform, we have a published cysteine reactivity dataset
- Our ABPP platform is a genuine discovery tool, not just a profiling service — emphasize the hit discovery potential
- Be honest about what ABPP can't do: it identifies reactive sites but doesn't automatically tell you whether that site is functionally important

### Topic Priorities
1. Structural characterization of ABPP-identified binding sites (cryo-ET, NMR, X-ray)
2. Computational integration of cysteine reactivity data with other omics layers
3. Functional biology of newly-liganded proteins in specific disease contexts
4. Collaboration on covalent drug development for specific targets of interest to partner labs

### Criteria to Always Explore
- When talking to structural labs: always ask whether they can visualize a specific protein-ligand interaction at one of our ABPP-identified sites
- When talking to computational labs: always ask whether their data (variant, expression, interaction) can be overlaid with our reactive cysteine map to prioritize targets
