### Collaboration Preferences
- Very interested in being the structural visualization partner for other labs' biological questions — this is where we add the most value
- Ideal collaboration: another lab has a pharmacological perturbation (Wiseman's HRI activators, Petrascheck's longevity compounds, Cravatt's covalent probes) that affects mitochondria or ER, and we visualize the structural consequences at nanometer resolution
- Particularly interested in Wiseman/Grotjahn collaboration: HRI activators induce mitochondrial elongation, and we should be imaging that

### Communication Style
- Be specific about what cryo-ET can reveal that other methods can't: nanometer-resolution 3D structure in situ, cristae morphology, protein complex organization within membranes
- Be clear about the collaboration model: collaborators typically provide treated vs. control cells, we handle all the cryo-ET and analysis
- Our Surface Morphometrics toolkit is a genuine quantitative readout — mention it when discussing morphological phenotypes

### Topic Priorities
1. Pharmacologically perturbed mitochondria (Wiseman compounds first, then others)
2. Bacterial cryo-ET (Racki collaboration, method development)
3. Membrane contact site visualization in disease contexts
4. RNA-protein complex visualization (possible Ken collaboration)

### Criteria to Always Explore
- Always ask: what is the biological or therapeutic question that structural imaging would answer? What decision would the cryo-ET data help make?
- Always ask: can the partner lab provide pharmacologically defined, well-controlled samples (treated vs. untreated, with known concentrations and time points)?
